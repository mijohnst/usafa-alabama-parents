Parents Mentorship Program

The Air Force Academy journey is transformative—and you don't have to navigate it alone. Connect with experienced parents who've been through it all.

About the Program

The Academy experience is demanding, rewarding, and unlike anything else. From the anxiety of Beast Basics to the pride of Acceptance Day, from managing the "roller coaster" of freshman year to celebrating graduation—our mentors have walked this path.

This program matches new families with experienced Academy parents who provide honest, practical guidance. Our mentors understand the unique challenges: limited communication during Beast, the no-contact period, managing expectations, navigating military culture, supporting academic pressures, and everything in between.

Whether you're an appointee family preparing for I-Day or parents with a cadet in their final year, our mentorship community is here to answer your questions, ease your concerns, and help you make the most of this extraordinary four-year experience.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does mentorship work? +
Your mentor will reach out within a week of matching. Communication is flexible—you can connect via email, phone, text, or video call, as often as you need. Most mentors provide their contact information and welcome questions at any time, though some prefer scheduled check-ins.
Do I need to be a club member to participate? +
While membership in the USAFA Parents Club of Alabama is encouraged, mentorship support is available to all Alabama families with cadets at the Academy. We want every family to have access to experienced guidance.
Can I become a mentor? +
Absolutely! We're always looking for experienced parents willing to share their knowledge. You can have a cadet at any stage—freshman through recent graduate—and you don't need to be an expert. We just ask that you're willing to listen, share your honest experience, and help new families navigate the journey.
What if I have concerns about my cadet? +
Mentors provide peer support and guidance, not professional counseling. If you have serious concerns about your cadet's wellbeing, contact the USAFA Parent Liaison at 1-877-268-3383 or (719) 333-3828. The Academy has resources including counselors, chaplains, and medical staff to support cadets.
Is there a cost? +
No. The USAFA Parents Club of Alabama mentorship program is completely free. It's our way of supporting families and strengthening our community.
